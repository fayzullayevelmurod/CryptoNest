What return would $1000 invested in cryptocurrency a year ago bring today?

The year 2022 is remembered by investors for the severe drop in the cryptocurrency market. It came after assets such as bitcoin (BTC) and Ethereum (ETH) set record high prices in 2021. In 2023, traders faced the aftermath of a prolonged “cryptozyme.” However, 2024 marked a recovery for the industry, and patient investors were ultimately left with the upper hand.

How would the different assets in your portfolio behave? In 2023, the total cryptocurrency market capitalization increases to $870 billion, suggesting a 110% year-over-year growth. According to a report from Binance, assets rose 55%, or $596 billion, in the fourth quarter. Although there was already enthusiasm in the market, it was too early to definitively say that the bull rally had begun.

Industry analysts are now unanimously saying that the industry is recovering. Until there is another collapse with another subsequent recovery. The crypto market is volatile, there is nothing you can do about it. Those who ignore the frenzied hype and cries of “everything is gone” make money on it. Let's try to calculate how much last year's investments brought to a seasoned investor.

Suppose a trader diversified his portfolio with five coins: bitcoin (BTC), Ethereum (ETH), Pepe (PEPE), FLOKI (FLOKI) and PAAL AI (PAAL). He invested $1,000 in each of them. The bitcoin investment definitely paid off, as the coin appreciated 126% over the course of a year. In the end, the return amounted to $2,260.

An investment in ETH could fetch about $1,780, which corresponds to an increase of 78% in a year. Holders of the PEPE token had particularly impressive returns, rising more than 800%, multiplying the initial investment amount to $9,000. FLOKI, too, showed growth in 2024, raising capital to $7,650, representing 665% of revenue.
